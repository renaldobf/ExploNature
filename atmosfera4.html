This is the lowest part of the atmosphere, is the part we live in. Contains most of our weather, clouds, rain, snow.

The temperature gets higher with altitude because of the increasing amounts of ozone. The ozone layer within the Stratosphere absorbs harmful ultraviolet rays of sunlight.

The coldest parts of our Atmosphere are located in this layer that can reach -90Â°C. The air is far too thin to breathe.

High-energy x-rays and UV radiation from the sun are absorbed in this layer. Many satellites orbit Earth within the Thermosphere. The quantity of energy coming from the sun exerts a powerful influence on the height of the top of this layer and the temperature within it.

The upper layer of our Atmosphere, where atoms and molecules escape into space, is called the Exosphere.
